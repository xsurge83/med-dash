Using cardiac output for training

In the world of athletic training, heart rate monitoring is commonly utilized, but stroke volume is the preferred measure of cardiac function as heart rate is a poor measure of energy expenditure. Athletes undergo stress testing several times a year to deduce their fitness level, a process typically involving VO2 (oxygen consumption) and lactate threshold measurements, which provide an estimate of maximum cardiac output.



The Wave provide those measurements outside of clinical settings while exercising. Most individuals heart rate range is alike, and therefore the main component which is trainable and can change is stroke volume (the amount of blood the heart push out every contraction). As individual gets fitter stroke volume increases resulting in lower heart rate at rest. Moreover, stroke volume is a non linear function as exercise intensity increases. More fit individuals will reach higher exercise intensity in endurance sport.
