Why Cardiac Output

Cardiac output refers to the volume of blood pumped by the heart each minute. It is arguably the single most important health assessment which can be obtained from a person. As all tissues are critically dependent on adequate blood perfusion to maintain normal function, a measure of cardiac output provides insight into the degree of tissue or organ system failure. However, cardiac output (defined as left ventricular stroke volume multiplied by heart rate) has, until now, been a very difficult measure to obtain. Historically, only invasive, and therefore costly and dangerous, procedures were available. Correspondingly, surrogate measures of cardiac output, such as blood pressure, body temperatures, and heart rate, have been utilized to deduce cardiac output status.

Cardiac Measurement

Measuring cardiac output for long duration may result in information that can be utilized for many different decisions making. Here, we share some thoughts in how the InfraSonic cardiac monitor device could be utilized in the field and in medical application.
  • Medications affect the whole human body: we assume there is more positive effect than negative. As cardiac output is an excellent indication of a physiologic state it is important that cardiac output not decrease due to medication intake, which can create complications.
  • Monitoring cardiac output while sleeping can indicate the level of sleeping and if the subject has rested for the needed duration.
  • Prevention is always the best medicine. Changes in cardiac output may indicate symptoms before going into trauma.

Example

A subject who takes extensive amount of medication including beta blockers, against hypertension (high blood pressure). After 20 minutes his blood pressure drops to the normal range but suffer from cardiac insufficiency. He has normal heart rate, but very low stroke volume which drops his cardiac index (cardiac output over his size) to 1.5. The range of cardiac index is 2.5-4 at rest. Therefore, subject is fatigue and suffer from dizziness. In this case his medication should be corrected to fit his personal needs. The clinician will not see this reading at the clinic when patient is admitted. the clinician will see the initial reading that indicates hypertension.
