Cardiac output monitoring

We have developed a wearable cardiac monitoring device, capable of providing continuous cardiac output. The device recorded motion of the sternum at frequencies which are at the infrasonic range, just below the range of human hearing. We have shown that specific aspects of chest wall motions are highly correlated to stroke volume output of the heart. Confounding factors such as breathing motions, speech noise, and other impact type motions (such as would occur during non-impact exercise, like cycling, swimming, rowing, and etc.) will not interfere with the assessment.

Upcoming performance unit

Most humans can reach the same range of heart rat, from rest to maximum intensity, but the amount of blood the heart can push out each contraction (i.e. stroke volume) make the difference between an athlete and untrained individual. Stroke volume is the trinable part that is directly related to our fitness. Our clinical study with 100+ subjects in a range of professional athletes to untrained people while cycling is scheduled to start in November 2014. Our subjects will undergo a stress test, measuring VO2 (oxygen uptake), Lactate, and infrasonic cardiac parameters simultaneously.
